## Design Context

### Users
Event planners, couples, corporate bookers, and venues in Trinidad & Tobago seeking a premium mobile bar experience. They're booking for celebrations — weddings, corporate events, parties. Non-drinkers are also explicitly included via Prosecco Zero. They arrive at this site expecting to be impressed before they even pick up the phone.

### Brand Personality
**Warm. Celebratory. Winking.**

Luxury that knows it's fancy and occasionally winks about it. The brand voice is the life of the party — it doesn't shout, it charms. Think: a perfectly chilled glass already waiting for you when you walk in. Warm, not cold. Premium, not pretentious. Funny once per page. No more.

Three-word personality: **Refined. Warm. Festive.**

Emotional goal: *Arriving at a beautiful event where everything is already perfect. The room is warm. The glasses are full. You belong here.*

### Aesthetic Direction
**Art-Deco Warmth** — editorial precision meets Caribbean light.

- **Tone:** Cormorant structure, champagne warmth, one wink per page
- **Decoration:** Intentional — subtle grain texture, concentric ring accents, rising bubble motifs
- **Light/Dark:** Light-first (cream backgrounds). Two deliberate dark breaks: MetricsCounter and Footer.
- **Border radius:** None. Flat, sharp edges throughout — editorial luxury. Exception: circular elements (bubble rings, avatar) use `rounded-full`.
- **Anti-references:** No glassmorphism, no rounded card grids, no neon on dark, no gradient headings for "impact", no identical card layouts

### Typography
- **Display:** Cormorant Garamond — weights 300/400, italic for emphasis
- **Body/UI:** DM Sans — weights 300/400/500
- **Eyebrow labels:** `text-xs tracking-[0.3em] uppercase text-[var(--gold-deep)]` — system-wide pattern, never deviate
- **Scale:** See `DESIGN.md` for full clamp() type scale

### Color System
Gold + cream primary, rosé secondary (product-specific), near-black for dark sections.

Key rules:
- Never `--white` or `rgba(255,255,255,X)` as text on light surfaces
- `--rose` appears only in rosé product contexts + max 2 celebratory moments per page
- `--brand-wa` (WhatsApp green) is isolated to WhatsApp-specific UI only
- Gold gradient text only for italic emphasis spans
- See `DESIGN.md` for full token list

### Motion Language
Champagne physics: bubbles rise, entrances have weight, corks pop exactly once.

- **One playful beat per section maximum** — restraint makes personality land
- `--ease-luxury: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` for all entrances
- Always implement `useReducedMotion()` and `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`
- No bounce/elastic easing — real objects decelerate smoothly

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the luxury signal.** Gold only appears when it means something. A personality moment only lands because it's rare. More is never more.
2. **One wink per section.** The brand has warmth and humor — expressed through exactly one departure per section from straight luxury. Never more. Never zero.
3. **Editorial rhythm over uniformity.** Varied section backgrounds, asymmetric compositions, one dark chapter in a cream-dominant layout. Visual rhythm comes from contrast, not repetition.
4. **Champagne physics.** Everything that moves should feel like champagne: rising, effervescent, celebratory. Entrances have weight. Exits don't linger.
5. **Accessibility is non-negotiable.** WCAG AA at minimum. `--muted` text passes 4.5:1 on cream. Dark sections use `--muted-dark`. No white text on cream. Reduced motion always respected.
